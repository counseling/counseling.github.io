Activities: The activities folder includes activities that can be employed for peer-group counseling sessions for orphaned and vulnerable children. The activities included here have been screened for cultural fit for East Africa, specifically Uganda. However, they may be adaptable to different regions.

Researchers

Organizations that provide counseling for orphans and vulnerable children face a major obstacle with regards to evaluation: how can the groups know if their programs are improving children’s psychosocial well-being? Program evaluations are useful for such organizations in designing interventions and attracting funding. This document provides a framework that seeks to evaluate the efficacy of peer group counseling sessions at relatively low cost.

In the interest of containing costs, this framework does not use randomized evaluation. If an organization has a large research budget, we recommend using a randomized evaluation instead as it can more convincingly identify the causal influence of counseling on child well-being.

Our framework uses a modified Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire (SDQ) to gauge child wellbeing. The following metrics can also be used if evaluators feel they are better suited for a particular context:

  • Children’s Depression Inventory
  • Beck Youth Inventory
  • Becks’s Hopeless Scale
  • Acholi Psychosocial Assessment Instrument Depression Problems Subscale
  • Clinician Administered PTSD Scale
  • Mini-International Neuropsychiatric Interview
